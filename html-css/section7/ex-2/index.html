IRISET : S&T Centralized Training Institute

Setup in 1957 by the Ministry of Railways, Government of India, to cater to the specialized training needs of their staff and officers in the field of Railway Signaling and Telecommunication. The Institute today caters to the needs of the entire Afro-Asian region.

Located in the southern part of India in Secunderabad adjoining the capital city of Hyderabad in Telangana, the Institute is spread over about 28.3 hectares. IRISET provides initial as well as advanced training, theory as well as hands-on, in Railway Signaling and Telecommunications. It caters to the total training requirement of the Officers and the Supervisors of Signal and Telecommunication department of Indian Railways.

IRISET is in the approved list of ESCAP and UNDP. It provides training in Railway Signaling and Telecommunications to private and public sector enterprises as well. It also trains officials of Foreign Railways.

There are four hostels in the campus itself, one for officers and three for supervisors. There are laboratories for all the important areas of Railway Signaling & Telecommunication to provide hands-on training in the Institute. The lecture halls are equipped with modern teaching aids.
